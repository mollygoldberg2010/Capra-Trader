📖 Glossary
Stock
A stock is a tiny piece of ownership in a company — when you buy one, you become a small co-owner of that business.
Share
One single unit of a stock. If Apple has 1 billion shares and you own 1, you own one billionth of Apple.
Portfolio
Your collection of all the stocks you own — like a folder that holds all your investments in one place.
Risk
How much the value of your investment could go up or down. Higher risk means bigger swings in price, for better or worse.
Stop Loss
A price level where you decide to sell so you don't lose even more money if a stock keeps falling.
Target Price
The price at which you plan to sell and take your profit — your goal for how high you hope it will go.
Bull Market
A period when stock prices are generally rising and investors feel confident — like a bull charging forward.
Bear Market
A period when stock prices are falling and investors feel cautious or fearful — like a bear swiping downward.
Dividend
A payment some companies make to their shareholders — like a small bonus just for holding their stock.
Index
A list of many stocks grouped together to show how the overall market is doing — the S&P 500 is a famous example.
